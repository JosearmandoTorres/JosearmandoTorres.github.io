About

Politics,data, identity.

I'm a Political Science student at the University of Pennsylvania, with minors in Statistics and Data Science. My research centers on Latino public opinion, minority coalition politics, and racial identity in American electoral behavior.

I'm interested in when and why minority groups form political coalitions — and what stands in the way. My work combines survey experiments, observational data, and causal inference to study these questions.

I grew up on the South Side of Chicago. I'm a first-generation college student and a member of MEChA at Penn. I'm fluent in English and Spanish.

Research
Senior Thesis · In Progress
When Do Latinos Form Coalitions? Perceived Competition and Race Neutrality
Survey experiment examining the conditions under which Latinos support political coalitions with other minority groups. Focuses on perceived competition and race-neutral framing as key mechanisms.
Working Paper
Racial Resentment and Immigration Polarization Among Latino Americans
Examines how racial attitudes toward African Americans shape immigration policy preferences within the Latino electorate.
Working Paper · LNS 2006
Perceived Competition and Black-Latino Coalition Attitudes
Uses the Latino National Survey to examine how perceived economic competition with African Americans shapes political solidarity attitudes among Latino respondents.
Manuscript · ANES Data
Ambivalent Voters and Issue Salience in American Elections
Analyzes cross-pressured voters using ANES data, with attention to how issue salience resolves ambivalence at the ballot box.
